# Wiring between the Micro-Epsilon ILD1420 and the Renesas ISL3177E RS-422 transceiver

Wire the differential pairs crossed by function—sensor TX to transceiver receiver, and transceiver driver to sensor RX:

| ILD1420 wire | Sensor signal | ISL3177E |
|---|---|---|
| Grey | Tx+ | Pin 8, A |
| Pink | Tx− | Pin 7, B |
| Yellow | Rx− | Pin 6, Z |
| Green | Rx+ | Pin 5, Y |

The remaining connections are important:

| ISL3177E pin | Connection |
|---|---|
| Pin 1, VCC | 3.3 V only |
| Pin 2, RO | RP2350 GP1, UART RX |
| Pin 3, DI | RP2350 GP0, UART TX |
| Pin 4, GND | RP2350 GND and ILD1420 blue GND wire |

```text
ILD1420                         ISL3177E                 RP2350
Grey   Tx+  ────────────────── A  pin 8
Pink   Tx−  ────────────────── B  pin 7 ── receiver ── RO pin 2 ── GP1

Green  Rx+  ────────────────── Y  pin 5
Yellow Rx−  ────────────────── Z  pin 6 ── driver ──── DI pin 3 ── GP0

Blue   GND  ────────────────── GND pin 4 ───────────── GND
                                      VCC pin 1 ────── 3.3 V
```

Add a 100 nF ceramic decoupling capacitor directly between pins 1 and 4. A 120 Ω termination resistor between A and B near the ISL3177E is normally appropriate for the sensor-to-board receive pair, particularly with a longer cable.

Do not connect the ILD1420’s 24 V supply to ISL3177E VCC—the transceiver accepts only 3.0–3.6 V. Power everything down before connecting or disconnecting the RS-422 wiring.

The mappings come directly from the [Micro-Epsilon ILD1420 manual](https://www.micro-epsilon.com/download/manuals/man--optoNCDT-1420--en.pdf) and the [Renesas ISL3177E datasheet](https://www.renesas.com/en/document/dst/isl3170e-isl3171e-isl3172e-isl3173e-isl3174e-isl3175e-isl3176e-isl3177e-isl3178e-datasheet).
